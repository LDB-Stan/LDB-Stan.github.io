This is the tale of a journey

from here

to here.

From skilled tradesman to data analyst? That would likely be an unusual occupational transition for most. Fair enough; I’m an unusual individual.

A lifelong career in the roles of machinist, tool and die maker, and a few leadership positions, has led me to many interesting and useful skills. Not all of them involve just the set-up and operation of machinery. There are procedures, orders of operations, inspection and quality control, flow of production, troubleshooting, and time management, to name a few, all while adhering to ISO certification standards and guidelines and following Kaizen and 5S lean manufacturing methodologies. This is just in performing the duties of a toolmaker or machinist.

The leadership positions brought even more useful skills, such as coordinating team efforts, taking measures to help streamline processes, and leveraging resources for optimal outcomes.

Identifying both real and potential problems and inefficiencies, as well as those brought to me by my team, and then working toward and implementing solid resolutions, was where I found my groove.

Quite simply, I’m into solving problems.

While the machine tool trade has been my livelihood for so many years, it's time for a change. This time I’m looking for something in which problem solving is the primary focus. In exploring the various options, data science, more specifically, data analysis, is what feels like home.
